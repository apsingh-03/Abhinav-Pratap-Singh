About

I am a dedicated academic researcher with over 5 years of experience in microelectronics and semiconductor device engineering. Currently serving as a Visvesvaraya Post-Doctoral Fellow at Panjab University, Chandigarh, I focus on designing and characterizing innovative optoelectronic devices.

My research interests span quantum dots, organic-inorganic perovskites, photodetectors, and emerging neuromorphic computing architectures. I combine TCAD simulation tools (SILVACO ATLAS, Cadence Virtuoso) with experimental validation to develop next-generation semiconductor devices.

With 21 peer-reviewed publications and strong teaching experience at both IIT (BHU) and UIET, I am committed to advancing microelectronics research while mentoring the next generation of engineers and scientists.

Research Interests

Microelectronics

VLSI design, semiconductor device physics, and integrated circuit fabrication with focus on miniaturization and performance optimization.

Photodetectors

UV-Visible broadband photodetectors, spectrum-tunable detectors, and self-powered photodiodes with enhanced responsivity.

Quantum Dots

Synthesis and characterization of colloidal quantum dots, perovskite QDs, and their heterostructure applications.

Neuromorphic Computing

Hybrid CMOS-Memristor systems, neuromorphic architectures, and emerging computing paradigms for AI acceleration.

Hybrid CMOS-Memristor Neuromorphic Computing Systems
Current Research | UIET, Panjab University
Designing hybrid neuromorphic architectures integrating CMOS circuitry with memristive devices. Using SILVACO ATLAS TCAD for device-level simulations and Cadence Virtuoso for circuit design and optimization. Focus on energy-efficient AI accelerators and bio-inspired computing paradigms.

Pb-Free Perovskite Quantum Dots Based Spectrum-Tunable Photodetectors
Completed Research
Development of lead-free, composition-engineered organic-inorganic perovskite quantum dots for flexible, spectrum-tunable photodetectors. Material synthesis via ligand-assisted re-precipitation (LARP), thin-film fabrication on flexible substrates, and comprehensive device characterization for UV-visible broadband detection.

Polymer and Doped Quantum Dots Blended Tandem Solar Cells
Completed Research
